A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was one of India’s most respectedscientists and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, TamilNadu, he came from a humble background and rose to become a key figure in India’s space and missile programs.He played a major role in India's nuclear tests in 1998, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India". He worked with organizations like DRDO and ISRO, contributing significantly to the development of ballistic missiles and satellite launch vehicles.Dr. Kalam was known not just for his scientific achievements, but also for his inspirational personality. He was a beloved teacher, writer, and visionary, especially passionate about igniting young minds. He passed away on 27 July 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. His life continues to inspire millions across the world.
